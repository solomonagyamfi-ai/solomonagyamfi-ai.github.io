01 · Flagship training case study

Ghana Adventures: Off-Season Booking Strategy

An end-to-end analysis of booking, revenue, seasonality, customer loyalty and sales-channel data designed to improve August–November demand.

Business challenge

Identify when demand was weakest, which customers and channels offered the strongest opportunity, and how the company could grow bookings without depending on deep discounts.

Process

Cleaned and typed the data, created core measures, compared monthly demand, package revenue, channel share and customer value, then built a Power BI dashboard.

Challenge overcome

Revenue alone could lead to weak product decisions, so recommendations also considered margin, capacity, customer feedback and possible duplicate package names.

Business outcome

Recommended customer retargeting, digital campaigns, experience bundles, group packages and loyalty benefits focused on the off-season.

02 · Professional application

HR & Operations Reporting

A confidentiality-safe view of how analytical thinking supports recurring workforce and operational reporting in a corporate environment.

Business challenge

Bring information from attendance, satisfaction, attrition, fleet, fuel, servicing and maintenance activities into clearer management summaries.

Process

Validated source records, organised categories and periods, calculated relevant indicators and structured findings for monthly and quarterly review.

Challenge overcome

Operational records can be inconsistent and sensitive. The approach prioritised validation, controlled definitions and confidentiality.

Business outcome

Produced more structured reporting that supports workforce discussions, cost monitoring and operational planning.

Data ethics: Employer records, individual employee information and confidential values are intentionally excluded from this public portfolio.

03 · Individual learning project

Customer Orders: Data Cleaning

Prepared an order dataset for reliable analysis by resolving quality issues in a repeatable Power Query workflow.

Business challenge

Prevent null values, inconsistent fields, invalid types and duplicate records from weakening later reports.

Process

Profiled columns, reviewed null patterns, standardised fields, corrected types, checked duplicates and documented transformations.

Challenge overcome

Missing data was not automatically replaced with zero because zero may have a genuine business meaning. Treatment depended on the field and context.

Business outcome

Created a cleaner, analysis-ready structure that improves confidence in totals, averages, categories and dashboard results.

04 · Individual learning project

FreshMart Ghana: Business Questions with SQL

Used structured queries to answer practical sales and product-performance questions in a retail-style scenario.

Business challenge

Convert transactional records into outputs that could support product, sales and inventory discussions.

Process

Reviewed the schema, selected relevant fields, wrote focused queries, validated aggregations and added plain-language interpretations.

Challenge overcome

A technically correct query is not automatically useful. Clear aliases, purposeful ordering and decision-focused explanations improved usability.

Business outcome

Demonstrated reliable use of filtering, joins, aggregations, CASE, HAVING and top-N analysis for commercial questions.

05 · Individual technical project

DataPulse: Reproducible Python Analysis Setup

Established and validated a project-specific Python environment for dependable analytics work.

Technical challenge

Packages were available globally but not inside the virtual environment used to run the project.

Process

Verified the active interpreter, installed dependencies in the correct environment, ran validation checks and tracked requirements.

Challenge overcome

Separated environment validation from analysis code so dependency problems could be diagnosed clearly.

Technical outcome

Created a reproducible foundation for future data preparation, exploratory analysis and visualisation.
